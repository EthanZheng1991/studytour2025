About the Study Tour

In January 2025, Ethan, as an AIDMer, participated in a study tour in London, UK, alongside professors and other fellow students from the School of Communication at HKBU, lasting over ten days.
During the trip, Ethan not only visited historical landmarks and toured world-renowned universities, but also visited Warner and the European headquarters of CNN. It was an unforgettable experience, marking a highlight in Ethan's life as a graduate student at HKBU.

Where History Meets Modernity

Big Ben, the iconic symbol of Britain, naturally caught my eye again and again. However, the place I visited more than once was the British Museum.
Since our accommodation was nearby, I loved wandering through its halls whenever I had free time. It houses a stunning collection of the world's finest artifacts. The Chinese relics on display, in particular, filled me with deep reflection and emotion.

Top Universities Steeped in History

On this trip, I visited the University of Cambridge and the University of Oxford with two professors. I saw Newton’s apple tree, walked across the bridge immortalized in Xu Zhimo’s poetry, and passed by the Corpus Clock unveiled by Stephen Hawking, with its distinctive grasshopper. Both Cambridge and Oxford have their own versions of the Bridge of Sighs, each with a unique style. Though we were merely passing through, we could still feel the profound academic spirit that lingers in the air.

Storytelling, Beyond Just Media

The three-day learning experience at CNN’s European headquarters was a key part of this study tour. We actively participated in the entire process of television news production. I was even fortunate enough to sit at the anchor desk and experience what it’s like to be a CNN news anchor. CNN Academy also invited seasoned media professionals to share their career experiences with us. It was truly an enriching and rewarding experience.

Following Masterpieces, Experiencing Europe

Beyond the British Museum, I also visited the National Gallery, the V&A Museum, the Sherlock Holmes Museum, and Madame Tussauds—iconic destinations filled with European cultural IPs. What excited me the most was seeing many original masterpieces, such as Van Gogh’s paintings. Being immersed in art added a touch of elegance to this London journey.
